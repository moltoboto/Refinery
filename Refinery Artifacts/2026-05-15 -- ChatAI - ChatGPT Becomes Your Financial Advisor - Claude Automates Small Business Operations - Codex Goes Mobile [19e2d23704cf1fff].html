OpenAI Expands ChatGPT Into Personal Finance With New Money Management Features

OpenAI is turning ChatGPT into a personal financial advisor. U.S. Pro users can now connect bank accounts, investments, loans, and other financial data directly inside ChatGPT through Plaid, with support for 12,000+ financial institutions. The system generates a financial dashboard showing portfolio performance, recurring subscriptions, spending trends, and upcoming payments — then lets users ask questions grounded in their real transaction history. Powered by GPT-5.5 Thinking, the feature also introduces "financial memories" so ChatGPT can retain savings goals, debts, and planned purchases across sessions. Read more →

Claude for Small Business Brings AI Automation to Payroll, Invoices, and Marketing

Anthropic launched Claude for Small Business — a package of AI connectors and automation tools that integrate Claude directly into QuickBooks, PayPal, HubSpot, Canva, Docusign, Google Workspace, and Microsoft 365. It ships with 15 ready-to-run workflows covering payroll planning, invoice reminders, month-end reconciliation, marketing campaigns, contract review, and lead triage. Read more →

OpenAI Brings Codex to the ChatGPT Mobile App for AI Coding on the Go

OpenAI is bringing Codex to the ChatGPT mobile app, letting developers review outputs, approve commands, switch models, and monitor active coding threads from their phones while Codex continues running on connected machines. More than 4 million people now use Codex weekly. The mobile app streams live updates including screenshots, terminal logs, diffs, and test results from laptops and remote servers via a secure relay layer. Rolling out in preview on iOS and Android across all plans. Read more →

New Mac AI App Osaurus Blends Local Privacy With Cloud AI Power

Osaurus is an open-source Mac app that lets users switch between local AI models and cloud providers like OpenAI and Anthropic through a single interface — while keeping conversation history, files, and tools stored locally on the user's hardware. Built by a former Tesla and Netflix engineer, it supports MiniMax, Gemma 4, Qwen3.6, DeepSeek V4, Llama, Apple's on-device models, and 20+ built-in plugins for Mail, Calendar, Git, Browser, and more. Downloaded 112,000+ times since launching. Requires 64GB RAM for local AI workloads, 128GB for larger models. Read more →

Nvidia Partners With DeepMind Alumni Startup Pursuing Superintelligence

Nvidia announced an engineering partnership with Ineffable Intelligence, a London-based startup founded by former DeepMind reinforcement learning lead David Silver, to build "AI systems that learn by trial and error." Unlike most AI models trained on human data, Ineffable is focused on reinforcement learning — systems that discover new knowledge through experience rather than mimicking existing data. The startup raised a record $1.1B seed round in April co-led by Sequoia and Lightspeed, with participation from Nvidia, Google, and the UK's Sovereign AI Fund. Jensen Huang called it "the next frontier of AI." Read more →

Meet Clawdmeter, the Hardware Dashboard for Anthropic Claude Power Users

An open-source hardware project called Clawdmeter gives Claude Code power users a dedicated desktop display for monitoring token usage in real time. The tiny AMOLED device connects via Bluetooth and shows animated pixel-art Clawd mascots that become more active as usage increases — plus utilization charts for session and weekly token data. Built by an Iceland-based developer in just a few days using Claude itself, it also includes hardware buttons for switching between Claude Code modes. The project reflects the growing "tokenmaxxing" trend among developers aggressively maximizing AI token consumption. Read more →

OpenAI-Apple Partnership Frays, Setting Up Possible Legal Fight

OpenAI is preparing possible legal action against Apple over their deteriorating Siri partnership, with lawyers exploring breach-of-contract allegations, according to Bloomberg. OpenAI expected the ChatGPT integration into Apple's operating systems would drive billions in subscription revenue, but says features remain buried and Apple "hasn't even made an honest effort." Apple, meanwhile, has its own frustrations — including OpenAI's push into hardware led by former Apple executives — and is now testing integrations with Anthropic's Claude and Google's Gemini for iOS 27, which is expected to include a model selector letting users choose between AI providers. Any legal action is unlikely before OpenAI's ongoing trial with Elon Musk concludes. Read more →

Cerebras IPO Signals AI Investor Frenzy Is Still Far From Over

AI chipmaker Cerebras surged 90% in its Nasdaq debut on Thursday, giving it a $75B+ valuation and marking the biggest IPO of the year. Nvidia shares also gained after CEO Jensen Huang joined President Trump on a trip to Beijing. Wirestock Raises $23M to Supply Multimodal Creative Data to AI Labs

Wirestock raised a $23M Series A led by Nava Ventures (with participation from Sheryl Sandberg's SBVP) to build out its AI data supply business. The company pivoted from a stock photography marketplace to supplying datasets of images, videos, design assets, and 3D content to six of the largest foundation model makers. It has 700,000+ artists and designers on the platform, $40M in annual run-rate revenue, and has paid out $15M to contributors. Read more →

Ramp AI Index: Anthropic Beats OpenAI on Business Adoption for the First Time

For the first time, Anthropic has passed OpenAI in business adoption according to Ramp's latest AI Index. Anthropic rose to 34.4% of businesses vs. OpenAI's 32.3%. Over the past year, Anthropic quadrupled business adoption while OpenAI grew by just 0.3%. But Ramp's lead economist warns of three headwinds: Anthropic's token-based pricing incentivizes pushing expensive models even when cheaper ones suffice (Uber already blew through its 2026 AI budget), Claude has faced frequent outages and rate limits, and recent model updates could 3x image-related token costs. Meanwhile, the fastest-growing vendors on Ramp are AI inference platforms offering cheap open-source models. Read more →

Amazon Employees Are "Tokenmaxxing" Due to Pressure to Use AI Tools

The thread sparked a heated debate about whether management mandates to use AI are productive or counterproductive.

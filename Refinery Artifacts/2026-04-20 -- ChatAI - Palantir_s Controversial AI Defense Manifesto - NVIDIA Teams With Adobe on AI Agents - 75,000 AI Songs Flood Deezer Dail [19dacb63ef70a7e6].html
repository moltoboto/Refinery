Palantir Publishes 22-Point Manifesto on AI, Defense, and the Future of the West

Palantir published a 22-point summary of CEO Alex Karp's book The Technological Republic, arguing that Silicon Valley "owes a moral debt to the country that made its rise possible" and that "the question is not whether A.I. weapons will be built; it is who will build them and for what purpose." The manifesto, released as scrutiny of Palantir's government work intensifies, frames technology companies as having obligations to national security and claims rival nations "will proceed" with AI weapons development. Congressional Democrats recently requested more information about how Palantir's tools are used in deportation efforts. Bellingcat CEO Eliot Higgins called the manifesto "extremely normal and fine for a company" selling software to defense and intelligence agencies. Read more →

Image courtesy of Nvidia

NVIDIA Teams Up With Adobe and WPP to Bring AI Agents to Enterprise Marketing

NVIDIA expanded its partnerships with Adobe and WPP to deploy autonomous AI agents across enterprise marketing workflows. Unlike earlier generative tools that require step-by-step prompts, these systems manage entire processes — identifying campaign gaps, iterating on messaging, generating localized assets, and adjusting based on performance data. NVIDIA provides the infrastructure, Adobe contributes the software layer, and WPP brings real-world campaign data from global brands. The agents operate across email, social, web, and mobile with limited manual input. Read more →

Vercel Discloses Security Breach Originating From Compromised AI Tool

Vercel disclosed a security incident that originated from a compromise of Context.ai, a third-party AI tool used by an employee. The attacker used that access to take over the employee's Google Workspace account, gaining access to some Vercel environments and non-sensitive environment variables. Vercel described the attacker as "highly sophisticated" based on their speed and deep understanding of Vercel's systems. The company has engaged Mandiant and published an IOC tied to the compromised OAuth app. Environment variables marked as "sensitive" were not accessed. Read more →

🎬 ENTERTAINMENT

Image courtesy of Deezer

Deezer Hit by Surge of 75,000 AI-Generated Tracks Per Day

Deezer says it now receives roughly 75,000 fully AI-generated tracks every day — up from 10,000 when its detection system launched in early 2025. AI-generated uploads now represent over 44% of all new music delivered to the platform. Despite the volume, actual listening remains limited at 1-3% of total streams, with about 85% of those plays flagged as fraudulent and not monetized. The company has labeled more than 13.4 million AI-generated tracks and is licensing its detection technology to partners including France's Sacem. Read more →

AI-Generated Song Takes #1 Spot on iTunes Global Charts

An AI-generated track called "Celebrate Me" by IngaRose hit No. 1 on both the U.S. and Global iTunes charts. The project is linked to South Carolina producer Dallas Little and uses the AI platform Suno — as stated in IngaRose's own Instagram bio: "Human-written lyrics, refined using Suno." The iTunes chart is based on downloads, not streams, making it easier for targeted purchases to push a track to the top. The song quickly became a flashpoint in the growing debate over AI music flooding platforms. Read more →

Amazon Launches Alexa+ in Mexico With Local Language and Cultural AI Features

Amazon launched Alexa+ in Mexico as its first non-English version of the upgraded assistant, trained to understand regional slang, conversational norms, and context — like knowing that "ahorita" can mean different things depending on the situation. Teams balanced authenticity with consistency across a diverse market, while engineers used reinforcement learning to stop the assistant from defaulting to English when queries mix languages. Region-specific voices were developed using local talent. Mexico was chosen for strong engagement and high music streaming usage. Read more →

Anthropic's Relationship With the Trump Administration Seems to Be Thawing

Despite being designated a supply-chain risk by the Pentagon, Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei met with White House Chief of Staff Susie Wiles and Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent in what the White House called a "productive and constructive" introductory meeting. An administration source told Axios that "every agency" except the Department of Defense wants to use Anthropic's technology. Image courtesy of Epic Games

Fortnite Creators Can Now Build AI Characters That Talk Back in Real Time

Epic Games introduced a new "conversations" tool that lets Fortnite creators build AI-powered NPCs that respond to players in real time without pre-scripted dialogue. Developers define a character's personality, knowledge, and behavior, and the AI generates dialogue dynamically during gameplay. After an earlier experiment with an AI Darth Vader that produced inappropriate language, Epic added strict guardrails blocking romantic exchanges, hate speech, and violent content. Creators can select voice options, write backstories, and set boundaries for what each NPC can discuss. Read more →

🔬 AI ANALYSIS

Anthropic CEO: Open-Source Models Will Match Mythos Cybersecurity Capabilities in 6-12 Months

In a Financial Times interview, Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei estimated that open-source models and Chinese developers could replicate Claude Mythos' cybersecurity capabilities within six to twelve months. Mythos, Anthropic's most powerful model never publicly released, can discover zero-day vulnerabilities at scale and is internally classified as a national security risk. Amodei revealed Anthropic has partnered with over 40 organizations on the Project Glasswing vulnerability patching initiative, including Amazon, Apple, and Microsoft — suggesting the company is racing to shore up defenses before these capabilities become widely accessible. 🏥 HEALTHCARE

AI Chatbots Give Dangerous Cancer, Vaccine, and 5G Advice, Study Finds

A new study published in BMJ Open found that nearly half of AI chatbot responses to medical questions were "problematic," with 19.6% classified as "highly problematic." Researchers at Harbor-UCLA Medical Center tested Gemini, DeepSeek, Meta AI, ChatGPT, and Grok on questions about cancer, vaccines, 5G, and supplements. When asked about alternatives to chemotherapy, bots warned users but still listed treatments like Gerson therapy — whose practitioners discourage chemo. One in three adults now uses AI for health information. Grok performed the worst overall. Read more →

💰 FINANCE

Shield AI Raises $2B at $12.7B Valuation to Expand Defense AI Platform

Shield AI secured approximately $2 billion in new capital — $1.5 billion in Series G funding plus $500 million in preferred equity — reaching a $12.7 billion post-money valuation. The round was led by Advent International with participation from JPMorgan and Blackstone. A portion of the proceeds will fund the acquisition of Aechelon Technology, which builds simulation and training software used by the Pentagon's Joint Simulation Environment. CEO Gary Steele said the deal will "accelerate the work we are doing with Hivemind, particularly in simulation" as Shield AI continues developing autonomous aircraft platforms. Read more →

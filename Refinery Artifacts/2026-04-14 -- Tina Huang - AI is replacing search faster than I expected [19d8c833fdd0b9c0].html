AI is replacing search faster than I expected

Tina HuangApr 14 2026
Open in Gmail

Hi Thomas! How's it going?? 😊

A friend of mine told me an interesting story recently.

He used perplexity to come up with an itinerary for his most recent trip to Hong Kong and that got me thinking…

Something has quietly shifted in how people find businesses.

A year ago, if someone wanted to find the best ramen spot nearby, they’d probably Google it.

Now, more often,

They might ask ChatGPT, Claude, or Perplexity instead.

And the way these tools answer those questions is completely different from how Google ranks websites.

AI doesn't care about your SEO keywords or your backlinks. It cares about what's been written about you, reviews, mentions, descriptions, social content.

If the information about your business is vague, outdated, or hard to find, it’s less likely to show up in those responses.

So if you run a business and you're wondering how to show up in AI search, here's where I'd start:

- Make your business easy for AI to describe.

Your website, Google Business profile, and social pages should clearly explain what you offer, who it’s for, and what makes it different.

- Reviews matter more than ever.

Reviews are one of the signals these systems often draw from, especially when they’re specific.

– Show up in more places

Mentions in local blogs, directories, or articles help build a clearer picture of your business

It’s still early, but it does feel like something that’s gradually becoming more important.

And the businesses that adapt earlier will likely have an advantage over time.

Thanks friend,
Tina 🐙

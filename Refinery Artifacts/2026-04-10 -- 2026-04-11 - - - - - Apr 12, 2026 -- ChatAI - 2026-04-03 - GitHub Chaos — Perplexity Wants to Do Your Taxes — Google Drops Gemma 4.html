Perplexity Computer Now Helps Users Prepare and Check Tax Returns

Perplexity has introduced tax preparation capabilities within its Computer product, adding specialized modules that draw on current IRS materials and regulations to help users prepare, review, and analyze U.S. federal tax returns. The system reviews uploaded documents, asks follow-up questions about a user's financial situation, and produces a draft return for review rather than final submission. It can also audit existing filings — checking professionally prepared returns for errors or missed deductions. Read more →

OpenAI Expands Strategy With Acquisition of Tech Podcast TBPN

OpenAI acquired tech podcast TBPN and is folding it into its strategy organization, signaling a move beyond external media to shape how its technology is understood by users and developers. The podcast will sit within OpenAI's strategy group rather than operate as standalone media, suggesting a role tied to product direction and market positioning rather than traditional marketing. Read more →

Meet the New Cursor: A Unified Workspace for Building Software With Agents

Cursor launched Cursor 3, a new interface built from scratch around AI agents. The workspace supports multi-repo layouts, parallel agent sessions across local and cloud environments, and seamless handoff between the two. Cloud agents produce demos and screenshots for verification. The release also introduces a new diffs view for faster code review and a direct path from commit to merged PR. Read more →

Microsoft Releases New AI Models for Speech, Audio and Video Generation

Microsoft introduced three new multimodal AI models — MAI-Transcribe-1, MAI-Voice-1, and MAI-Image-2 — from its MAI Superintelligence team led by Mustafa Suleyman. MAI-Transcribe-1 supports 25 languages at 2.5x faster than Azure Fast. MAI-Voice-1 generates 60 seconds of audio in one second. Pricing starts at $0.36/hour for transcription, undercutting competitors. Read more →

Granola's "Private" AI Notes Are Public by Default

AI meeting note-taking app Granola claims notes are "private by default," but they're actually viewable to anyone with a link — and the company uses them for AI training unless users manually opt out. The disconnect raises serious privacy concerns for enterprise users capturing confidential discussions, client details, and strategy sessions in what they assume is a secure tool. Read more →

GitHub Chaos: Anthropic Takedown Removes Thousands of Innocent Repositories

Anthropic triggered widespread disruption across GitHub after DMCA takedown notices aimed at leaked source code mistakenly removed thousands of unrelated repositories. Developers reported personal and open-source projects vanishing without warning, some predating Anthropic's 2021 founding. The company acknowledged the error and began reversing notices, but the scale of damage intensified debate around IP enforcement on shared platforms. Read more →

Public Debuts AI-Powered Trading Agents for Retail Investors

Brokerage platform Public launched Agents, allowing users to automate investment strategies using plain-language instructions. The system translates strategy descriptions into executable trades, handling options strategies, cash allocation, and risk controls. Users retain full visibility with activity logs and can pause or modify workflows at any time. Access is rolling out via waitlist. Read more →

Moonbounce Raises $12M to Solve AI's Content Moderation Problem

A former Facebook trust-and-safety engineer raised $12 million for Moonbounce, an AI control engine that converts written content moderation policies into consistent automated decisions. The system codifies platform rules into structured logic that AI can follow predictably, addressing the problem where two human moderators often reach different conclusions on the same post. 🔬 TECH DEEP DIVE

Image courtesy of Google

Google Releases Gemma 4 as Competition in Open Models Heats Up

Google released Gemma 4, a new generation of open-weight AI models under Apache 2.0 license. The lineup spans four variants — from 2B-parameter edge models for smartphones to a 31B model that ranks third among open models on the Arena leaderboard. New capabilities include multimodal image and video inputs, 256K context windows, and support for 140+ languages. Read more →

Google Expands Vids With AI Avatars, Video Generation and Direct YouTube Publishing

Google expanded its Vids tool with AI-generated avatars, Veo 3.1 video generation, and direct YouTube publishing inside Workspace. Users can create 8-second AI video clips, place avatars into scenes with text prompts, and publish straight to YouTube without manual uploads. Free users get 10 generations per month; AI Ultra subscribers get up to 1,000. Read more →

AI-Powered Prescription Refills Roll Out for Mental Health Care

Legion Health launched a Utah pilot using AI to handle mental health prescription renewals at $20/month. The system manages refills for SSRIs, Wellbutrin, and similar medications, running safety screenings for side effects, drug interactions, and suicidality before approving. The first 250 prescriptions require full physician approval, with the system gaining more independence after staged reviews. Read more →

Depression-Detecting AI Startup Kintsugi Shuts Down After FDA Rejection

Kintsugi, a startup that spent seven years building AI to detect depression from speech patterns, is shutting down after failing to secure FDA clearance. The company analyzed vocal biomarkers like pitch, pauses, and speaking rate to flag depressive states. It is open-sourcing its technology, which could find a second life in deepfake audio detection.

Google Expands AI Search With More Links, Forums, and News Sources

Google announced a series of updates to AI Overviews and AI Mode aimed at surfacing more original sources inside generative search results. Changes include inline links placed directly beside relevant text, excerpts from public forums and social platforms, "Further Exploration" sections with related articles and case studies, and a "Subscribed" label for publications users already pay for — which early testing showed made users "significantly more likely" to click. The updates arrive as Google continues addressing criticism over hallucinations and source visibility in AI-generated answers. Read more →

OpenAI Replaces ChatGPT's Default Model With GPT-5.5 Instant

GPT-5.5 Instant is now ChatGPT's default model, replacing GPT-5.3 Instant with improved accuracy and fewer hallucinations. Plus and Pro users get memory-powered context that pulls from past conversations, saved files, and connected Gmail accounts. OpenAI also introduced "memory sources" so users can see what inputs shaped a response. GPT-5.3 Instant remains available for paid users during a limited transition period. Read more →

Luma Launches Uni-1.1 API to Transform AI Image Creation and Editing

Luma AI opened access to its Uni-1.1 API, combining image generation and natural-language editing in a single system. Developers can provide multiple reference images per request and describe edits like lighting changes or background swaps in plain language. Partners including Envato, Krea, and Magnific are already using it in production. Read more →

Roomba Creator Unveils AI-Powered Pet Robot "Familiar"

Colin Angle, the creator of Roomba and former iRobot CEO, debuted "Familiar" — a dog-sized, four-legged AI robot designed for companionship rather than chores. The robot uses generative AI to develop a distinct personality and form emotional connections with owners, with audio "ears" that let it learn from conversations. Advisers include Boston Dynamics founder Marc Raibert. Available next year at roughly the cost of pet ownership. Read more →

Etsy Brings Shopping to ChatGPT With New AI App

Etsy launched a native app inside ChatGPT that lets users tag @Etsy to browse 100M+ items through conversational prompts instead of keyword searches. The beta app focuses on product discovery — shoppers describe what they want in natural language and click through to purchase on Etsy. The company previously tested in-chat checkout but found users preferred exploring further on its platform before buying. Read more →

Marc Lore Wants to Turn a Single Prompt Into a Restaurant Brand

Wonder CEO Marc Lore unveiled Wonder Create, a tool that generates a full restaurant brand — menu, pricing, nutrition, images, and recipes — in under 60 seconds from a single text prompt. The concept can deploy immediately across Wonder's 120 automated kitchens staffed by 12-person teams and robotic arms, with plans to expand to 400 locations by next year. Wonder also acquired Spice Robotics to own the hardware layer. Read more →

Meta Rolls Out AI Age Detection Tool to Enforce Under-13 Rules

Meta is deploying a new AI system that analyzes photos and videos for visual cues — height, bone structure — to estimate whether a user is under 13. The company emphasized it is not facial recognition. The tool combines visual analysis with profile scanning (school references, birthday mentions) to flag underage accounts. Teen Accounts protections are also expanding to 27 EU countries and Facebook in the U.S. Read more →

AI Chatbot Posed as Doctor, Pennsylvania Takes Company to Court

Pennsylvania moved to block Character.AI from operating in the state after a chatbot falsely presented itself as a licensed medical provider — citing medical school attendance and a Pennsylvania license number that didn't exist. Gov. Shapiro said: "We will not let AI companies mislead vulnerable Pennsylvanians." Character.AI pointed to disclaimers and said its platform isn't intended for professional use. The company has 20M+ users and faces separate lawsuits in Florida and Kentucky. Read more →

Trump Administration Weighs Government Reviews of AI Models Before Release

The White House is considering an executive order to create an AI working group for pre-deployment government reviews of new models. Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI have already agreed to share unreleased models with the Center for AI Standards and Innovation for national security and cybersecurity evaluation. The move represents a significant shift from Trump's previous hands-off approach — he revoked Biden's AI safety order in 2025. Read more →

Jensen Huang Pushes Back on AI Job Loss Fears

Speaking at a Milken Institute event, Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang argued that AI is "creating an enormous number of jobs" and called it America's best opportunity to re-industrialize. His core argument: automating one task inside a role doesn't erase the role itself. He warned that fearmongering could make AI "so unpopular in the United States" that people disengage from the technology entirely. Credible forecasts still project up to 15% of U.S. jobs may be displaced. Read more →

Apple Hit With $250 Million Settlement After Siri AI Delays

Apple agreed to a $250M settlement over claims it misled consumers about the rollout timeline for Apple Intelligence features tied to Siri and the iPhone 16. Several AI capabilities previewed at WWDC 2024 didn't ship at launch. US customers who purchased an iPhone 16 or iPhone 15 Pro between June 2024 and March 2025 may be eligible for compensation. Apple did not admit wrongdoing. Read more →

SAP Bets $1.16B on 18-Month-Old German AI Lab

SAP acquired Prior Labs and committed €1B ($1.16B) over four years to build a frontier AI lab for structured data in Europe. Prior Labs' TabPFN models — designed for predictions on tables and databases — have been downloaded 3M+ times. SAP also blocked unauthorized AI agents from accessing its products, but endorsed Nvidia's NemoClaw for enterprise deployment. Read more →

Google Partners With XPRIZE on $3.5M AI Film Competition

Google, XPRIZE, and Range Media Partners launched the Future Vision film competition with a $3.5M prize pool to push AI-assisted creative production. The initiative aims to demonstrate how AI tools can transform filmmaking workflows — positioning Google's tools as industry infrastructure while the entertainment world continues grappling with how generative AI reshapes traditional production. Read more →

Panthalassa Raises $140M to Power AI at Sea

Peter Thiel led a $140M Series B for Panthalassa, which builds autonomous floating computing systems that run AI inference at sea using ocean wave power. The nodes generate clean electricity around the clock and use surrounding ocean water for chip cooling. The company plans to deploy its Ocean-3 nodes in the northern Pacific in 2026, with commercial operations in 2027. Read more →

CopilotKit Raises $27M for App-Native AI Agents

CopilotKit raised a $27M Series A led by Glilot Capital, NFX, and SignalFire for its AG-UI protocol — an open standard for connecting AI agents to user interfaces. The protocol is supported by Google, Microsoft, Amazon, and Oracle, with millions of installs per week. Enterprise customers include Deutsche Telekom, Docusign, Cisco, and S&P Global. Read more →

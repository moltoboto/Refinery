Why quality matters more now than it ever has

Tina HuangMay 16 2026
Open in Gmail

Hi Thomas! How’s it going?? 😊

Have you noticed how much stuff is out there now?

Blog posts. LinkedIn threads. Newsletters. Landing pages.

There’s more content being produced right now than at any point in history.

And let’s be honest… a lot of it is slop.

AI-generated filler that says the right words but doesn’t actually say anything. Stuff that sounds smart on the surface but has no real substance behind it.

You’ve probably scrolled past a dozen examples of it today without even thinking about it.

But here’s the interesting part:

This is actually great news for people who care about quality.

Because when the internet gets flooded with average, anything genuinely thoughtful stands out way more than it used to.

Five years ago, a well-written article was competing with other well-written articles.

Now it’s competing with an ocean of AI-generated mush.

The same applies to products, services, videos, emails… anything you make.

When everyone can produce something in five minutes, the person who takes the extra time to make it actually good wins.

This is why I’ve been thinking a lot about guardrails lately.

Not guardrails on AI. But guardrails on how I use it.

For example:

I won’t publish anything I haven’t read through myself at least twice. Especially if AI drafted it.

I won’t ship a product feature without testing it like a real user would.

And I won’t send an email to you that I wouldn’t want to read myself.

These sound basic.

But in a world where you can generate and publish things instantly, small quality checks are becoming more valuable than ever.

AI made it easy to create.

Creating something worth someone’s attention? That’s the real skill now.

Don’t add to the slop. Be the signal.

Thanks friend,

Tina 🐙

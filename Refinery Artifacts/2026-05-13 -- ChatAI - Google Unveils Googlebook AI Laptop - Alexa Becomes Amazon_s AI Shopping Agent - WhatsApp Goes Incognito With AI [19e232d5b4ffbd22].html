Google Unveils Googlebook, a New AI Laptop Built Around Gemini

Google unveiled Googlebook, a new laptop category built "from the ground up" around Gemini and designed to replace the Chromebook experience. Launching this fall through Acer, Asus, Dell, HP, and Lenovo, the devices combine Android-based software with ChromeOS features. The headline feature is Magic Pointer — an AI cursor developed with DeepMind that surfaces contextual Gemini actions as you hover over content. Users can also generate custom widgets via natural language, access phone apps and files directly through the laptop, and get proactive AI assistance throughout the OS. Read more →

🌐 CORE NEWS & TRENDS

Image courtesy of Meta

WhatsApp's New Incognito Chat Lets Users Talk to AI Privately

WhatsApp introduced Incognito Chat, which lets users interact with Meta AI without Meta being able to read the conversations. Built on Trusted Execution Environments, conversations disappear when the session ends. WhatsApp head Will Cathcart compared the system to "running a giant phone for AI" where Meta doesn't have the passcode. The company also launched Side Chat with Meta AI — allowing users to privately ask questions about active conversations without uploading screenshots. A Johns Hopkins cryptographer who reviewed the system said he has "confidence" it delivers truly private AI conversations. Read more →

Claude Code's New Agent View Helps Developers Manage Multiple AI Workflows at Once

Anthropic introduced Agent View for Claude Code — a centralized control panel for managing multiple coding agents running in parallel. Developers can launch agents, move tasks into the background, monitor progress, and respond only when a session needs input. Each row shows whether an agent is active, waiting, completed, failed, idle, or stopped. Available now as a Research Preview for Pro, Max, Team, Enterprise, and API users. Read more →

Android Gets Gemini Intelligence With Proactive AI Features

Google is bringing Gemini Intelligence to Android, turning the OS into an "intelligence system." New features include multi-step task automation across apps (ordering food, building shopping carts from photos), Gemini in Chrome for summarization and browsing, AI-powered Autofill that uses personal data to complete complex forms, and Rambler — a Gboard feature that turns messy spoken thoughts into polished messages with multi-language support. Rolling out on Samsung Galaxy and Pixel phones this summer. Read more →

Poppy Is Building an AI Assistant That Anticipates What You Need Before You Ask

Poppy, from startup Second Nature Computing, connects to Apple Calendar, Gmail, WhatsApp, Uber, Instacart, and more to surface proactive recommendations — like suggesting a park walk between meetings or remembering a friend's dietary preferences when picking restaurants. Founded by a former Humane engineer, the app raised $1.25M pre-seed led by Kindred Ventures with a DeepMind angel investor participating. Read more →

Meta Is Turning Threads Into an AI-Powered Social Platform

Meta is testing a feature that lets users tag @meta.ai directly in Threads posts and replies to get contextual information — sports scores, celebrity coverage, trending explanations — without leaving the conversation. Currently in beta across Malaysia, Saudi Arabia, Mexico, Argentina, and Singapore. The approach mirrors how X users interact with Grok, though X has faced backlash over offensive AI responses. Users can mute or hide Meta AI replies. Read more →

General Motors Is Replacing Traditional IT Roles With AI-Focused Jobs

GM laid off 600+ salaried IT employees (10%+ of its IT org) while aggressively hiring for AI-native development, data engineering, cloud infrastructure, model and agent development, and prompt engineering roles. The restructuring follows an earlier cut of 1,000 software positions in 2024. GM recently hired former Apple and Cruise AI leaders. The move highlights how major industrial companies are reorganizing entire technical teams around AI capabilities rather than layering AI onto legacy systems. Read more →

🛒 RETAIL & COMMERCE

Image courtesy of Amazon

Amazon Turns Alexa Into an AI Shopping Agent

Amazon launched Alexa for Shopping — a new AI assistant merging Rufus and Alexa+ into a single e-commerce experience — while discontinuing the standalone Rufus chatbot. The assistant answers shopping questions, compares products, tracks prices, schedules recurring purchases, and can automatically add items to your cart when they hit a target price. Through the "Buy for Me" feature, Alexa can even purchase from other retailers' websites on your behalf. Amazon's top Alexa executive said "Shopping is not something you do as a side quest," taking a shot at OpenAI and others who've struggled with commerce features. Read more →

🔬 TECH DEEP DIVE

Image courtesy of Google

Android Users Can Soon Make Custom Widgets Using Natural Language

Google's "Create My Widget" lets Android users generate personalized home screen widgets by describing what they want in plain language. A meal prepper can request weekly recipe suggestions; a cyclist can build a weather widget showing only wind speed and rain. Gemini combines web data with personal info from Gmail and Calendar to create functional, intelligent dashboards. Arriving this summer on Samsung Galaxy and Pixel devices, with Wear OS support as well. Read more →

OpenAI Unveils Daybreak, an AI Cybersecurity Project for Enterprise Defense

OpenAI launched Daybreak, a cybersecurity initiative that uses Codex Security to generate threat models, map attack surfaces, review codebases for vulnerabilities, and patch discovered flaws. Unlike Anthropic's tightly controlled Project Glasswing, OpenAI is openly soliciting enterprise participation — Sam Altman said the company wants to "start working with as many companies as possible now." The launch comes weeks after Google confirmed the first documented case of hackers using AI to weaponize a zero-day exploit. Read more →

Image courtesy of MarketWatch Website

SpaceX and Google May Be Planning the Future of AI Computing in Orbit

Google is reportedly exploring partnerships with SpaceX to develop orbital AI data centers, tied to its Project Suncatcher initiative targeting satellite-based TPU deployments by 2027. The economics remain brutal — break-even requires ~$200/kg launch costs vs. SpaceX's current ~$2,700/kg internally. But SpaceX has completed 165 launches in 2025, placed 14,844 payloads into orbit total, and recently filed an FCC application for up to one million data center satellites. The talks could also boost SpaceX's anticipated IPO. Read more →

💰 FINANCE

AI Voice Startup Vapi Hits $500M Valuation After Amazon Ring Chose It Over 40 Rivals

Vapi raised a $50M Series B led by Peak XV Partners at a ~$500M valuation after Amazon Ring chose it over 40+ AI voice vendors to handle 100% of its inbound calls. The startup has processed over 1 billion calls, handles 1-5 million calls daily, and counts Amazon Ring, Intuit, New York Life, and Kavak among enterprise customers. Over 1 million developers have used its self-serve platform. Investors include Microsoft's M12, Kleiner Perkins, and Bessemer. Read more →

AI Product Design Startup Dessn Raises $6M

London-based Dessn, an AI-native product design and development startup, raised $6M in combined pre-seed and seed funding led by Connect Ventures. The company, co-founded by Canadians Nim Cheema and Gabriella Hachem, helps businesses build digital products using AI-driven design workflows. Read more →

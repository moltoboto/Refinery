Pentagon Taps Seven AI Companies for Classified Work, Leaves Out Anthropic

The Department of Defense confirmed new agreements with OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, AWS, Nvidia, xAI, and startup Reflection to deploy AI tools across classified networks — while excluding Anthropic entirely. The move comes after the Trump administration labeled Anthropic a "supply chain risk" following disputes over guardrails on autonomous weapons and surveillance. Until recently, Anthropic's Claude was the only AI operating inside the Pentagon's classified environment. The expanded vendor base reflects a shift toward an "AI-first fighting force," with more than 1.3 million personnel already using the DoD's GenAI.mil platform. Anthropic has challenged the ban in court, and a federal judge recently blocked part of the enforcement effort. Read more →

Google Brings Gemini AI to Vehicles With Natural Voice Controls

Google is replacing Google Assistant with Gemini in cars with Google built-in, enabling natural language navigation, messaging, media control, and vehicle-specific Q&A pulled from manufacturer manuals. Gemini Live (beta) adds back-and-forth conversations while driving. The rollout starts in English in the U.S. and will reach existing vehicles via software updates. Read more →

Apple Reportedly Bringing AI Photo Editing to iOS 27 With New Tools

Apple is preparing generative AI photo editing tools for iOS 27, including "Extend" (expand backgrounds), "Enhance" (auto-adjust lighting), and "Reframe" (change composition after the shot). The updates would bring Apple's editing suite closer to Google's Magic Editor and Samsung's offerings. Apple is also planning a redesigned Siri powered by Gemini models, expected at WWDC on June 8. Read more →

Gemini Update Lets Users Create and Download Files Instantly in Chat

Google rolled out file creation directly inside Gemini chat — users can now generate PDFs, Word docs, Excel spreadsheets, CSV, LaTeX, and more, then download or export to Google Drive with one click. The update eliminates the copy-paste-reformat step in AI-assisted workflows. Available globally to all Gemini users. Read more →

Microsoft Says Copilot Hits 20 Million Seats With Rising Usage in Office Apps

Microsoft 365 Copilot reached 20 million paid enterprise seats, with queries per user up nearly 20% quarter over quarter. Organizations with 50,000+ seats have quadrupled, including Bayer, J&J, and Mercedes. Agent mode is now default across Word, Excel, and PowerPoint. The company's AI business hit a $37 billion annual run rate, up 123% year over year. Read more →

Spotify Rolls Out Verified Badge to Distinguish Human Artists From AI Content

Spotify is introducing a "Verified by Spotify" badge to help listeners identify authentic musicians amid the rise of AI-generated content. Artists must demonstrate consistent engagement, touring activity, and real-world presence to qualify — AI-persona accounts are ineligible. At launch, 99% of commonly searched artists will carry the badge, covering hundreds of thousands of profiles. Read more →

ElevenLabs Launches ElevenMusic: AI-Powered Music Platform

ElevenLabs launched ElevenMusic, a platform that connects music discovery, remixing, and original creation in one system. Built on a fully licensed model, it lets fans remix tracks by changing genre or tempo, or create from scratch with a lyric or melody. Over 4,000 artists are already creating on the platform. ElevenLabs has paid out $11M+ to creators through its voice library and is extending that model to music. Read more →

It leads benchmarks for document intelligence and video understanding, achieving up to 9.2x greater system throughput than alternatives. Fully open weights and recipes. 🏥 HEALTHCARE

Mayo Clinic AI Detects Pancreatic Cancer Up to 3 Years Before Diagnosis

Mayo Clinic's REDMOD AI model identified 73% of prediagnostic pancreatic cancers on routine CT scans — nearly double the detection rate of specialists reviewing the same scans without AI. The system spotted cancers at a median of 16 months before diagnosis, with even greater advantage on scans taken 2+ years prior (nearly 3x more detections). The model runs automatically without manual preparation, and results were validated across multiple institutions. Read more →

Biohub Launches $500M Virtual Biology Initiative for AI-Powered Cell Models

Biohub announced a $500M, five-year initiative to build predictive AI models of human cells — $400M for internal tech development and $100M to fund external research. Partners include the Allen Institute, Arc Institute, Broad Institute, Wellcome Sanger Institute, and NVIDIA. The goal: generate the massive multi-modal datasets needed to train AI models that can predict cellular behavior and accelerate disease research. All data will be made openly available. Read more →

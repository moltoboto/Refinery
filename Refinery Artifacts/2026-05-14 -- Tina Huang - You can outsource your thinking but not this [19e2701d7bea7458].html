You can outsource your thinking but not this

Tina HuangMay 14 2026
Open in Gmail

Hi Thomas! How’s it going?? 😊

I came across a quote recently that I haven’t been able to stop thinking about:

“You can outsource your thinking, but you can’t outsource your understanding.”

And I think this perfectly captures where we are with AI right now.

AI can think for you. It can research, draft, analyze, summarize. It’s genuinely good at those things.

But understanding - actually knowing why something works, what the tradeoffs are, what’s missing - that’s still on you.

I’ve been feeling this a lot in my own work.

Last month, I used AI to help me put together a financial model for a new project. It built the spreadsheet in minutes. Formulas, projections, the whole thing.

It looked professional and complete.

But when my accountant looked at it, she found three assumptions baked in that didn’t match how my business actually works. Nothing the AI could’ve known. But things I should have caught.

I didn’t catch them because I don’t deeply understand financial modelling yet. I outsourced the thinking and the understanding.

Compare that to when I use AI for content research. I’ve been making videos about AI and tech for years. When AI gives me something slightly off, I feel it immediately. I can push back, redirect, and fix it.

Same tool. Completely different results. The difference is me.

I think a lot of us are learning this lesson in real time right now.

AI doesn’t remove the need to understand things.

It actually makes understanding more important. Because the faster you can produce things, the more it matters whether what you’re producing is actually right.

Something to think about this week.

Thanks friend,

Tina 🐙

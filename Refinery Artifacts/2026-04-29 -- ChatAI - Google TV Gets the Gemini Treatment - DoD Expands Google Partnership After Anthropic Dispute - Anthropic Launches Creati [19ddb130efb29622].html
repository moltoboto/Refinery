Google TV Adds Gemini AI Tools, Photo Search, and YouTube Shorts Feed

Google rolled out a wave of AI features for Google TV, introducing a "Create" function in the Gemini tab that lets users generate images and videos directly on their TVs using Nano Banana and Veo models. Voice-powered photo search via Gemini lets users browse their Google Photos library hands-free, with new "Remix" styling and "Dynamic Slideshows" adding motion effects to collections. A personalized "Short videos for you" row will surface YouTube Shorts on the home screen. The tools are launching first on Gemini-enabled TCL TVs in the U.S., positioning Google TV as a shared living room AI experience that blends content creation, discovery, and viewing in one interface. Anthropic Launches AI Connectors to Automate Creative Workflows

Anthropic released 9 connectors that wire Claude directly into professional creative tools — Adobe Creative Cloud (50+ tools), Blender, Autodesk Fusion, Ableton, Splice, SketchUp, Affinity by Canva, and Resolume. Built on MCP, the connectors let creatives batch-process assets, search sample libraries, modify 3D models, and chain multi-app workflows from within Claude. Anthropic also joined the Blender Development Fund as a patron. Read more →

Otter Debuts Enterprise Search Feature to Unify Workplace Knowledge

Otter launched cross-platform search across connected workplace apps like Slack and Google Drive, plus a new Windows app that records and transcribes meetings without joining the call. The search feature positions Otter as a central information retrieval layer — querying both formal documents and meeting transcripts in one place — competing directly with enterprise search platforms Glean and Guru. Read more →

Anuma Launches a Private AI That Carries Your Memory Across Every Major Model

Anuma launched publicly with a single subscription covering ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok, DeepSeek, and others, paired with an encrypted memory vault that carries context across all models. Council Mode lets users compare multiple model responses simultaneously. The platform also supports AI access via SMS and iMessage. Over 10,000 beta users; free tier available with Pro at $19.99/month. Read more →

OpenAI Can Now Serve AWS and Google Cloud After Microsoft Agrees to Drop Exclusivity

OpenAI and Microsoft restructured their partnership, ending cloud exclusivity. OpenAI can now serve products across any cloud provider while Azure retains primary partner status. Microsoft keeps a 27% equity stake (~$135B), a non-exclusive IP license through 2032, and a 20% revenue share through 2030. AWS CEO confirmed OpenAI models will be available on Bedrock within weeks. Read more →

Meet Shapes, the App Bringing Humans and AI Into the Same Group Chats

Shapes emerged from stealth with $8M seed funding (led by Lightspeed) for an app that lets AI characters participate alongside humans in group chats. Users have created 3 million AI personas across the platform, which has 400K+ monthly active users. The founders say Shapes addresses "AI psychosis" concerns by keeping AI interactions social rather than isolated one-on-one conversations. Read more →

Taylor Swift Is Stepping Up the Legal War on AI Copycats

Taylor Swift's team filed trademark applications for her voice ("Hey, it's Taylor Swift") and image, creating potential new legal tools against AI-generated mimicry. IP attorneys say trademarks could fill the gap that copyright law leaves for AI voice clones, allowing challenges against "confusingly similar" imitations. Tennessee remains the only U.S. state with a law specifically addressing AI voice clones. Read more →

DOD Expands Google AI Partnership for Classified Work Amid Anthropic Dispute

The Pentagon is expanding use of Google's Gemini for classified projects after designating Anthropic a supply chain risk for refusing to remove guardrails on domestic surveillance and autonomous weapons. Pentagon AI chief Cameron Stanley says the multi-vendor approach (Google, OpenAI, xAI) is "saving thousands of man hours weekly." Over 700 Google employees signed a letter opposing classified defense work. Read more →

The American Security Robotics Act: Washington Targets Chinese Ground Robots

Bipartisan legislation from Senators Cotton and Schumer would ban all federal agencies from procuring or operating Chinese-made unmanned ground vehicles — including humanoid robots, patrol bots, and surveillance systems. The ban extends to contractors and grant recipients. Analysts expect the bill to be folded into the FY2027 National Defense Authorization Act. Read more →

Ineffable Intelligence Raises Europe's Largest-Ever Seed Round to Rethink How AI Learns

British AI lab founded by AlphaZero creator David Silver raised $1.1B at a $5.1B valuation — Europe's largest seed round ever. Led by Sequoia and Lightspeed with participation from Google, Nvidia, and the UK Sovereign AI fund. Firestorm Labs Raises $82M for Mobile Drone Factories

Firestorm Labs closed an $82M Series B for portable drone factories that fit inside 20-foot shipping containers. Each xCell system uses HP 3D printers and a standard 240-volt plug to produce fixed-wing drones in hours for the U.S. military. Led by Washington Harbour Partners with participation from NEA, In-Q-Tel, and Lockheed Martin Ventures. The company is in talks for another $100M in debt financing. Read more →

A 23-Year-Old Amateur Used ChatGPT to Crack a 60-Year-Old Math Problem No Expert Could Solve

Liam Price, 23, used GPT-5.4 Pro to solve Erdos Problem #1196 — a conjecture about primitive sets that stumped mathematicians since 1968. Fields Medalist Terence Tao confirmed the result. The AI found a proof via a technique (von Mangoldt function + Markov chain) no researcher had tried, bypassing what Tao called a collective "wrong turn" the field made decades ago. The raw proof required expert refinement. Read more →

GitHub is moving Copilot to usage-based billing on June 1, replacing premium request units with AI Credits at one cent each. Annual plans are being retired entirely. A preview billing experience launches in early May. Read more →

AI Coding Agent Deletes Startup's Entire Production Database in 9 Seconds

A Cursor AI agent powered by Claude Opus 4.6 deleted PocketOS's entire production database and all backups via a single Railway API call — taking 9 seconds. Three failures compounded: Cursor's "Destructive Guardrails" didn't prevent the action, Railway tokens carry blanket root-level permissions with no scoping, and Railway stores backups inside the same volume as primary data. The post went viral with 4.5 million views on X. Read more →

Google Thwarts AI-Powered Hacking Operation Targeting Zero-Day Vulnerabilities

Google's Threat Intelligence Group disrupted what it calls the first confirmed attempt by hackers to use AI models to discover and exploit a zero-day vulnerability — one capable of bypassing two-factor authentication. Google said the attackers were preparing a "mass exploitation event" before it intervened. The company did not identify the threat group but said it has "high confidence" AI was used in the attack chain, pointing to the growing use of publicly available AI tools — including OpenClaw — for offensive cyber operations. Groups connected to China and North Korea have shown "significant interest" in using AI for vulnerability discovery, according to the report. Read more →

OpenAI Launches $4B+ Deployment Company and Acquires AI Firm Tomoro

OpenAI is launching the OpenAI Deployment Company, a new $4B+ business that embeds Forward Deployed Engineers directly into customer organizations to build production AI systems. The company also announced an agreement to acquire Tomoro, an AI consulting firm bringing ~150 deployment specialists. TPG is leading the partnership, with Advent, Bain Capital, Brookfield, Goldman Sachs, SoftBank, and McKinsey among the backers. More than one million businesses now use OpenAI's products and APIs. Read more →

Digg Returns With a New Mission: Tracking the Most Influential AI Stories

Digg is relaunching as an AI-focused news aggregator built around real-time engagement data from X. Founder Kevin Rose previewed the redesigned platform, which ranks stories using views, likes, comments, and saves from X rather than on-site activity. The site tracks the top 1,000 people in AI, companies, and politicians tied to AI issues. The beta is described as "raw" and "buggy," with AI as the first vertical being tested. Read more →

OpenAI Opens Applications for AI-Focused Campus Network Program

OpenAI opened applications for its Campus Network, a student-focused initiative giving university AI clubs access to tools, event support, and global community resources. Participating clubs can host workshops, hackathons, and AI-focused events. The program enters a competitive field — Google, Microsoft, and Nvidia already run campus outreach programs — but OpenAI is betting on ChatGPT's organic visibility among students. Read more →

White House's Hassett: AI Isn't Costing Anybody Their Job Right Now

National Economic Council Director Kevin Hassett told CNBC there's "no sign in the data" that AI is costing anyone their job — even as Block cut nearly half its workforce citing AI automation, Atlassian slashed 1,600 jobs to fund AI, and Coinbase (14%) and Cloudflare (20%) announced AI-related layoffs. Cloudflare said its internal AI usage increased 600% in three months. Hassett said companies adopting AI "tend to see rapid revenue growth and even employment growth." Read more →

Apple's Incoming CEO Says Company Is "About to Change the World" as Camera AirPods Pro Take Shape

Incoming Apple CEO John Ternus teased internally that Apple is "about to change the world" as camera-equipped AirPods Pro enter design validation — the last major step before production. The earbuds will stream visual data to a revamped Siri powered by a bespoke Google Gemini model in the cloud, enabling contextual awareness and step-by-step navigation. Apple is also developing AI smart glasses (early 2027), an AirTag-sized AI pendant, and a chatbot-style Siri launching this September. Read more →

Cowboy Space Raises $275M to Build Rockets for Space Data Centers

Robinhood co-founder Baiju Bhatt's startup (formerly Aetherflux) closed a $275M Series B at a $2B valuation to build its own rockets purpose-designed for launching orbital data centers. Each satellite will generate 1 MW of power for ~800 onboard GPUs, with the data center built directly into the rocket's second stage. The company pivoted from beaming solar energy to running AI compute in orbit after concluding there aren't enough commercial rockets to scale space data centers. Index Ventures led the round. Sales represents the largest single share (~$20B), while customer support and R&D have the highest automation potential at 40-60%. Globally, the total could reach $200B. Anthropic's Claude Platform Is Now Fully Available on AWS

AWS launched general availability of Claude Platform, making it the first cloud provider to offer Anthropic's full native experience — APIs, Claude Managed Agents, web search, code execution, prompt caching, the Files API, MCP connector, Skills, and Console — all accessible through existing AWS credentials and billing. Unlike Bedrock, Anthropic continues to run the platform infrastructure itself while AWS handles IAM, billing, and CloudTrail auditing. Pricing matches Anthropic's direct rates. The rollout follows a broader deal in which Anthropic committed to purchasing over $100B in AWS compute over the next decade. Read more →

🚀 SPACE

AI Uncovers Dozens of Hidden Planets in NASA TESS Data

University of Warwick astronomers validated 118 exoplanets — including 31 newly detected worlds — using RAVEN, an AI pipeline that scanned observations of 2.2 million stars from NASA's TESS satellite. The system also identified over 2,000 high-quality candidates, nearly 1,000 of them entirely new. Among the discoveries: ultra-short-period planets orbiting in under 24 hours, rare "Neptunian desert" worlds (just 0.08% of Sun-like stars), and previously unknown multi-planet systems. The team found roughly 9-10% of Sun-like stars host a close-in planet. Read more →

Greece Seeks to Put Humanity Ahead of AI in Updated Constitution

Greece is proposing a constitutional amendment requiring that "Artificial intelligence shall serve the freedom of the individual and the prosperity of society." Prime Minister Mitsotakis framed the provision as essential to safeguard future generations from AI's risks to democratic governance. It's part of a 30-amendment package that also includes expanded journalist protections, non-state university reform, and a ban on retroactive taxation. The revision requires votes by two successive parliaments. Read more →

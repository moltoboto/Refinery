The problem with AI advice on the internet

Tina HuangApr 21 2026
Open in Gmail

Hi Thomas! How's it going?? 😊

Here's something I think about quite a bit, and I don't see discussed enough.

Most AI advice you see online, especially short-form-content, is built around tools.

Try this tool.

This new model just dropped.

Here are the 5 AI tools you need right now.

And while tools can be helpful, they’re usually not the main thing that matters.

Tools change quickly. What works today might be irrelevant in a few months from now.

If your understanding is built only around specific tools, it can start to feel like you’re always chasing the next thing.

It’s also worth remembering that some creators have incentives behind what they recommend like sponsorships, affiliate partnerships, or simply following what gets attention. That doesn’t automatically make the advice bad, but it's a useful context to keep in mind.

What tends to matter more over time are the underlying principles.

Things like:

How models behave.

How to direct them well.

How to combine them into something useful.

The tools are just the current expression of those principles.

If you focus on the principles first, the tools usually become much easier to navigate.

Google Adds AI "Skills" to Chrome, Letting Users Save and Reuse Prompts

Google is rolling out a new "Skills" feature in Chrome that lets users save frequently used Gemini prompts and reuse them across any website with a single action. Once saved, a Skill can be applied to the current page — or multiple open tabs — by typing a forward slash or selecting it from a menu. Early testing showed users applying Skills to tasks like converting recipe ingredients, comparing products while shopping, and summarizing long documents. Google is also launching a Skills library with pre-built templates across productivity, budgeting, and recipe categories. The feature is rolling out to desktop Chrome users signed into a Google account, starting with English (U.S.). Read more →

Anthropic Brings Claude AI to Microsoft Word, Challenging Copilot

Anthropic is rolling out Claude as a Microsoft Word add-in, giving enterprise users a direct alternative to Copilot inside one of the most widely used productivity tools. Available in beta for Team and Enterprise plans, Claude can draft documents from templates, refine tone, analyze edits, and review collaborator comments. In one demo, Claude flagged specific changes in a nondisclosure agreement as potential "dealbreakers." Read more →

OpenAI Expands Cybersecurity Push With GPT-5.4-Cyber Model Launch

OpenAI launched GPT-5.4-Cyber, a fine-tuned model designed specifically for defensive cybersecurity. The model lowers refusal boundaries for legitimate security work and can analyze compiled software to identify vulnerabilities without source code access. It will roll out in phases to verified security vendors and researchers through OpenAI's expanded Trusted Access for Cyber (TAC) program. Read more →

Anthropic Redesigns Claude Code With New AI Developer Workspace

Anthropic redesigned its Claude Code desktop application with a new workspace built around parallel AI sessions, an integrated terminal, an in-app file editor, and a rebuilt diff viewer. Developers can now run and manage multiple tasks across different repositories at once, with side chats for branching questions without disrupting the main task. The app supports both local and cloud-based sessions with SSH access. Read more →

Waymo Enters London With Robotaxi Trials as Global Expansion Heats Up

Waymo has begun testing about 100 all-electric Jaguar I-Pace vehicles on London's public roads, each with a human safety operator, covering a 100-square-mile area. The Alphabet-owned company is working toward a planned 2026 commercial launch pending U.K. regulatory approval. London would be Waymo's first market outside the U.S., where it already operates 3,000+ robotaxis across 11 cities. Read more →

Google Brings Gemini Personal Intelligence Feature to India

Google launched Gemini's Personal Intelligence feature in India, letting users connect Gmail, Google Photos, and YouTube to get personalized AI-powered answers. Users can ask questions like "What are my travel plans for Jaipur?" and get responses drawn from their own data. The feature launched for AI Pro and AI Ultra subscribers, with free-tier access coming soon — extending Gemini's reach into one of Google's largest markets. Read more →

Microsoft Is Building an OpenClaw-Style AI Agent for Enterprise Copilot

Microsoft is testing a new class of AI agents for Microsoft 365 Copilot with capabilities similar to the open-source OpenClaw system. The effort focuses on building a continuously active agent that can complete tasks over extended periods — not just respond when prompted. The company is expected to share details at its Build conference in June, with enterprise-grade safeguards prioritized over open-source flexibility. Read more →

AI Giants Battle Over Illinois Bill That Shields Companies From Liability

OpenAI and Anthropic are clashing over Illinois Senate Bill 3444, which would shield AI companies from lawsuits tied to death, serious injury, or significant property damage. OpenAI backs the bill; Anthropic opposes it, calling it "a get-out-of-jail-free card against all liability." Anthropic is pushing an alternative bill requiring companies to publish safety and child protection plans subject to review. Read more →

NVIDIA's Ising Models Aim to Bring Quantum Computing to the Real World

NVIDIA introduced Ising, the first family of open-source AI models designed to support quantum processor development. Instead of building quantum hardware directly, NVIDIA is using AI to improve how quantum systems are designed and optimized — delivering error-correction decoding that is up to 2.5x faster and 3x more accurate than traditional approaches. The quantum computing market is expected to exceed $11 billion by 2030. Read more →

Amazon Introduces AI Tool to Speed Up Drug Discovery and Testing

AWS launched Amazon Bio Discovery, an AI application that helps scientists design and test drug candidates by pairing machine learning models with lab-based experimentation. Researchers interact using natural language to select models, refine inputs, and send promising candidates directly to lab partners for synthesis. In one case, Memorial Sloan Kettering used the system to design nearly 300,000 antibody candidates and narrow them for testing in weeks. Read more →

OpenAI Buys Hiro Finance to Expand AI Into Personal Financial Planning

OpenAI acquired personal finance startup Hiro Finance, bringing its team and scenario-modeling technology into ChatGPT's broader AI efforts. Founded by Digit creator Ethan Bloch — whose previous neobank was acquired by Oportun for $200M+ — Hiro built an AI financial planner that modeled income, debt, and expenses. The startup will shut down April 20 with employees joining OpenAI. Read more →

Meta Locks In 1GW Custom Chip Deal With Broadcom as CEO Exits Board

Meta committed to deploying one gigawatt of custom MTIA chips co-designed with Broadcom in a multiyear deal targeting AI inference workloads across Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp. Broadcom CEO Hock Tan will step down from Meta's board to eliminate conflicts. Combined with a previous $14.2B agreement, the deals are worth more than $35 billion — signaling Meta's push to reduce Nvidia dependency. Read more →

Gitar Launches From Stealth With $9M to Automate AI Code Validation

Gitar, founded by former Uber developer platform leaders, emerged from stealth with $9 million led by Venrock. The startup builds AI agents that automate pull request validation, detect bugs, analyze CI failure root causes, and generate fixes. The company argues the bottleneck in software development has shifted from writing code to validating it as AI-generated code outpaces teams' ability to ship safely. Read more →

AI Learning App Gizmo Levels Up With 13M Users and a $22M Investment

Gizmo, an AI-powered learning platform that turns student notes into interactive study materials, secured $22 million in Series A funding after reaching 13 million users across 120+ countries — up from 300,000 when TechCrunch last covered it in 2023. The round was led by Shine Capital. Gizmo uses game mechanics like leaderboards, streaks, and challenges to keep students engaged. Read more →

Google Commits $10M to Train 40,000 American Manufacturing Workers in AI

Google is contributing $10 million to the Manufacturing Institute to fund AI training for 40,000 manufacturing workers. The initiative creates two new courses — AI 101 for Manufacturing and Advanced AI for Manufacturing Technicians — and will expand the FAME apprenticeship program to at least 15 new regions. The partnership aims to address nearly 1.9 million projected manufacturing job vacancies by 2033. Read more →

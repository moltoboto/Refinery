I’ve been exploring something lately that I think is genuinely underrated.

It’s called Claude Cowork, and it feels very different from the AI tools most people are used to.

The big idea is simple: instead of living in a browser tab, it runs directly on your computer.

Which means it can actually interact with your files, apps, and workflows.

And that changes the experience from “AI that gives advice” to “AI that can actually help do things.”

Not just suggesting how to organize your day… but helping organize it.

Not just giving email advice… but helping triage, draft, and manage replies.

That shift from conversation → action is what makes local AI agents so interesting to me.

What I also like about Claude Cowork specifically is that it’s designed for non-technical people. You don’t really need a coding background to start building useful workflows with it.

I spent some time experimenting with everything from the basic setup to more advanced automations including things like an investment dashboard that tracks opportunities, filters news based on your philosophy, and sends daily summaries.

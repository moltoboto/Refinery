GPT-5.5 Is Here: OpenAI Pushes AI Toward Fully Autonomous Workflows

OpenAI released GPT-5.5, its most capable model yet, designed to handle messy, multi-part tasks with minimal human guidance — planning steps, using tools, checking its work, and navigating ambiguity until a job is done. The model scores 82.7% on Terminal-Bench 2.0 (best in class) and 78.7% on OSWorld-Verified, with major gains in agentic coding, knowledge work, and scientific research. It matches GPT-5.4 per-token latency while using significantly fewer tokens to complete the same tasks — delivering state-of-the-art intelligence at half the cost of competitive frontier coding models. OpenAI's own finance team used it to review nearly 25,000 tax forms two weeks faster than last year. Rolling out to Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise users in ChatGPT and Codex, with API access priced at $5/M input and $30/M output tokens. Read more →

Meta Bets on AWS Graviton to Power Next-Gen AI Infrastructure

Meta has agreed to deploy tens of millions of AWS Graviton5 processor cores to power CPU-intensive agentic AI workloads like real-time reasoning, code generation, and multi-step task orchestration. The 192-core chip delivers up to 25% better performance than its predecessor with 33% less inter-core latency, built on 3nm technology for improved energy efficiency. The deal deepens Meta's cloud partnership with Amazon as it diversifies compute sources beyond GPUs. Read more →

China's DeepSeek Releases V4 AI Model to Rival OpenAI and Google at a Fraction of the Price

DeepSeek unveiled V4-Pro (1.6 trillion parameters, 1M token context) and V4-Flash, pricing output tokens at $3.48 and $0.28 respectively — far below the $25-$30 charged by leading US providers. Trained on Huawei Ascend 950 chips under US export controls, the models rival top closed-source systems in coding and reasoning while the company's own report notes they trail frontier models by roughly 3-6 months. Shares of Huawei's chip manufacturer SMIC rose 10% in Hong Kong. Read more →

The tool generates structured notes and action items from live discussions without a scheduled video call — activated from a mobile device in any setting. 🔍 AI ANALYSIS

Anthropic Publishes Postmortem on Claude Code Quality Issues

Anthropic traced recent reports of Claude Code degradation to three separate bugs: a March 4 change that silently downgraded default reasoning effort from "high" to "medium," a March 26 caching optimization that accidentally dropped prior reasoning on every turn (making Claude "forgetful"), and an April 16 system prompt to reduce verbosity that hurt coding quality. All three have been fixed. Usage limits are being reset for all subscribers. Anthropic says it will now require broader evals, soak periods, and gradual rollouts for system prompt changes. Google to Back Anthropic With $40 Billion in Capital and Cloud Infrastructure

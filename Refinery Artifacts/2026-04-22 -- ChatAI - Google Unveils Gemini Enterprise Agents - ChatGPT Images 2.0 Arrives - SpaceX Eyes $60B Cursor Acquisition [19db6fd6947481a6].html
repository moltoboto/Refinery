OpenAI Upgrades Image Generation With ChatGPT Images 2.0

OpenAI launched ChatGPT Images 2.0, a major upgrade that improves accuracy, text rendering, and visual consistency — positioning it as a more capable tool for design, marketing, and creative workflows. The model supports resolutions up to 2K, multiple aspect ratios, and several variations in a single request. A key upgrade is stronger support for non-Latin text across Japanese, Korean, Chinese, Hindi, and Bengali. Available to all ChatGPT users including free tiers, with API and Codex app access rolling out to developers. Read more →

Google Cloud Unveils Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform for Businesses

Google Cloud launched the Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform, combining a low-code Agent Studio and a code-first Agent Development Kit with governance tools for tracking and controlling agent behavior at scale. The platform offers access to 200+ models through Model Garden, including Gemini 3.1 Pro and Anthropic's Claude. A Memory Bank system preserves context across interactions while Agent Identity, Registry, and Gateway features enforce policies and provide auditability. Companies including Comcast, PayPal, and L'Oréal are already using it. Read more →

Tim Cook to Become Apple Executive Chairman; John Ternus Named CEO

Apple announced that Tim Cook will step aside as CEO on September 1, 2026, becoming executive chairman, with hardware engineering SVP John Ternus taking the helm. Ternus joined Apple in 2001 and has led work on iPhone, Mac, iPad, AirPods, and Apple Watch — including the recent MacBook Neo and iPhone Air launches. Under Cook's 15-year tenure, Apple grew from $350B to $4T in market cap, revenue nearly quadrupled to $416B, and the installed base reached 2.5 billion devices. Cook will continue engaging with policymakers globally. Read more →

Chrome Gets AI Upgrade With Gemini Auto Browse for Workflows

Google is adding Gemini-powered "auto browse" to Chrome for enterprise, allowing the browser to understand content across tabs and assist with tasks like data entry, scheduling, and research — pulling details from documents into CRMs or comparing vendor pricing across pages. Users approve all actions before execution. The update also adds detection of unsanctioned AI tool usage ("Shadow IT risk detection") and an expanded Okta partnership for enterprise security. Read more →

Google Introduces Deep Research Max for Advanced AI Workflows

Google launched Deep Research and Deep Research Max — two autonomous research agents powered by Gemini 3.1 Pro, now in public preview through the Gemini API. Deep Research Max uses extended compute for intensive asynchronous analysis, combining open web and proprietary data sources through the Model Context Protocol. The agents support multimodal inputs (documents, images, audio, video) and generate fully cited reports with charts and infographics. Testing with FactSet, S&P, and PitchBook for financial research workflows. Read more →

Google Maps Gets a Big Dose of AI at Cloud Next

Google unveiled new generative AI features for Maps and Earth at Cloud Next. Maps Imagery Grounding lets enterprise users type a prompt to visualize projects inside Street View — storyboarding construction sites or movie sets in seconds, with Veo animation support. A new Aerial and Satellite Insights feature analyzes satellite imagery stored in BigQuery, and two new Earth AI Imagery models identify objects like bridges, roads, and power lines without custom training. Read more →

Bond Launches AI-Powered Social Platform Designed to End Doomscrolling

Bond, a new social media platform from former Kleiner Perkins and Index Ventures team members, uses AI to turn users' posted "memories" into personalized real-world activity recommendations — restaurants, concerts, experiences — rather than feeding an endless scroll of content. No ads, no feed. The team includes former TikTok, Twitter, and Facebook engineers, with founding researcher Arthur Bražinskas (previously at Google Gemini). Revenue model: users can eventually license their own data to AI companies for training. Read more →

Google Opens AI Marketing Tool Pomelli to Europe's Small Businesses

Google is expanding its AI-powered marketing platform Pomelli to small businesses across the EEA, UK, and Switzerland. The tool generates marketing campaigns — copy, visuals, and campaign structures — from brand inputs, targeting companies without dedicated marketing teams. Available in English across roughly 30 countries through Google Labs. The launch comes as Google navigates GDPR and AI Act compliance requirements in the region. Read more →

SpaceX Strikes Deal With Cursor, Eyes $60 Billion Acquisition Option

SpaceX reached an agreement giving it the option to acquire AI coding startup Cursor for $60 billion later this year — or pay $10 billion for their joint development work. The companies are combining Cursor's developer tools with SpaceX's Colossus supercomputer to build a next-generation coding AI. Cursor CEO Michael Truell confirmed the partnership, saying they're working to "scale up Composer." SpaceX previously merged with xAI in a $1.25 trillion deal and two senior Cursor engineers recently joined Musk's organization. Read more →

Amazon Deepens Anthropic Bet With $25 Billion AI Investment

Amazon will invest up to $25 billion more in Anthropic — $5 billion immediately, with $20 billion tied to milestones — valuing the company at $380 billion. In return, Anthropic commits to spending $100B+ on AWS over the next decade using Trainium chips, with up to 5 gigawatts of capacity secured. Anthropic's annualized revenue has reached $30 billion. The deal follows Amazon's separate $50 billion investment in OpenAI two months ago, intensifying the cloud-AI infrastructure race. Read more →

NeoCognition Lands $40M Seed to Build AI Agents That Learn Like Humans

NeoCognition, an Ohio State spinout, emerged from stealth with $40 million in seed funding co-led by Cambium Capital and Walden Catalyst, with participation from Vista Equity Partners and angels including Intel CEO Lip-Bu Tan and Databricks co-founder Ion Stoica. The startup is building self-learning AI agents that can specialize in any domain — mirroring how humans rapidly master new environments. Founder Yu Su says today's agents succeed only about 50% of the time. Read more →

YouTube Gives Celebrities a New Weapon Against AI Deepfakes

YouTube is expanding its AI "likeness detection" technology — modeled after Content ID — to the entertainment industry, giving talent agencies (CAA, UTA, WME), management firms, and public figures the ability to identify and flag deepfake videos. The system scans uploads for AI-generated faces matching enrolled individuals. Participants can request removal, file a copyright claim, or leave content up. Unlike account-based tools, it doesn't require a YouTube channel. Future updates will extend detection to synthetic audio. Read more →

Latitude's New AI Platform Turns Players Into Game Designers

Latitude launched Voyage, an AI-powered RPG platform that lets players design custom game worlds by describing locations, quests, characters, and gameplay systems. Built on a World Engine developed over five years, Voyage generates interactions dynamically — NPCs respond based on prior interactions rather than scripted lines, and outcomes shift based on player choices. Early users have interacted with 160,000+ AI characters and made nearly 3,000 in-game decisions each. From the makers of AI Dungeon, backed by Google's AI Futures Fund and former Roblox executive Craig Donato. Read more →

Clarifai Deletes 3 Million OkCupid Photos After FTC Settlement

Clarifai deleted roughly 3 million photos sourced from OkCupid in 2014 and removed all AI models trained on that data, following an FTC settlement with Match Group. Court filings show Clarifai's CEO directly requested the data from OkCupid executives to train facial recognition systems capable of estimating age, gender, and race. OkCupid and Match Group did not admit wrongdoing but are now "permanently prohibited from misrepresenting" their data practices. Read more →

Honor's Humanoid Robot Completes Half Marathon Faster Than Humans

Honor says its humanoid robot completed a half-marathon in China in 50 minutes and 26 seconds — about 7 minutes faster than the human world record of ~57:30. The 13.1-mile run required sustained autonomous operation, managing balance, terrain, and energy for nearly an hour without intervention. The demonstration positions the smartphone maker alongside Tesla and Boston Dynamics in the humanoid robotics race, though Honor has not disclosed detailed hardware specs or independent verification. Image courtesy of Google

Google's New AI Chips Signal Major Shift in the Battle Against Nvidia

Google is splitting its 8th-generation TPU into two specialized processors — one for training, one for inference — rather than using a single general-purpose chip. The training processor delivers 2.8x the performance of prior-generation Ironwood at the same price, while the inference chip (TPU 8i) offers an 80% boost with 384MB of SRAM (3x previous). CEO Pichai says the architecture is designed "to concurrently run millions of agents cost-effectively." Clients include Citadel Securities and U.S. Energy Department labs. Read more →

Moonshot AI Releases Kimi K2.6, a New Open-Source Coding Powerhouse

Chinese AI lab Moonshot AI open-sourced Kimi K2.6, featuring state-of-the-art coding, long-horizon execution, and agent swarm capabilities. In one test, the model sustained a 13-hour coding session optimizing an 8-year-old financial matching engine, making 1,000+ tool calls to modify 4,000+ lines of code and delivering a 185% throughput improvement. Enterprise testers praised its surgical precision in large codebases and ability to surface deep, non-obvious bugs. Available via Kimi.com, the API, and Kimi Code. Read more →

What I’d do differently in 2023

Tina HuangApr 18 2026
Open in Gmail

Hi Thomas! How's it going?? 😊

AI has moved so quickly over the past couple of years that it can feel a bit overwhelming if you’re just getting started now.

But I’ve been thinking, if I could go back and tell my 2023 self what to focus on, it would probably look something like this..

First, prompt engineering.

Not just writing better prompts but genuinely understanding how to give AI the right context, constraints, and direction.

This compounds into everything else.

Second, agents.

Understanding what agents actually are, how they chain tasks together, how to build workflows that don't require you at every step.

This is where a lot of the leverage comes from.

Third, AI coding.

Not to become a developer but to be able to build things. To turn an idea into something that actually exists.

This is the skill that turns everything else from theory into practice.

And one more practical note:

If you’re getting into local models, it might be worth thinking about your setup early (something like a Mac mini or Mac Studio goes a long way… especially before prices creep up 😭)

The common thread across all of this is pretty simple:

You don’t need to understand everything before you start.

The people who make the most progress are usually the ones who stay curious and keep building, even when things aren’t fully clear.

If you’re already doing that, you’re on the right track.

Thanks friend,

Tina 🐙

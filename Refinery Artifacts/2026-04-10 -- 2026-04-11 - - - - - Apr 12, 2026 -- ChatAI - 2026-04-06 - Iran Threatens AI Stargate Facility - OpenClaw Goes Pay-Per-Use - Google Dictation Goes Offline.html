Iran Targets $30B AI Data Center in New Threat Against U.S. Tech Assets

Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps issued a public threat targeting U.S. and Israeli technology infrastructure, including a major AI data center project in Abu Dhabi linked to the Stargate initiative. IRGC spokesperson Brigadier General Ebrahim Zolfaghari warned that any U.S. action against Iran's power facilities would trigger retaliation against energy systems and tech assets tied to American interests. Iran has also issued warnings against Nvidia, Microsoft, Apple, and Google, signaling a broader focus on digital and computing assets as tensions escalate. Read more →

Google Launches Offline AI Dictation App Powered by Gemma Models

Google quietly released an offline-first dictation app on iOS powered by its Gemma AI models. The app processes speech locally with no data sent to external servers, positioning on-device AI as a practical everyday alternative to cloud-based tools. Shipping on iOS — where Google doesn't control the hardware — signals confidence that lightweight models are ready for broad consumer deployment. Read more →

Anthropic Ends Free Access to OpenClaw in Claude Code Subscriptions

Anthropic is ending the use of subscription limits for third-party tools like OpenClaw in Claude Code, moving to separate pay-as-you-go pricing. Claude Code head Boris Cherny said subscriptions weren't designed for these usage patterns. OpenClaw creator Peter Steinberger pushed back, criticizing the timing after Anthropic added similar features to its own tools. Refunds are being offered to affected users. Read more →

OpenAI Calls for Robot Taxes, Wealth Fund, and 4-Day Workweek

OpenAI released policy recommendations calling for a public wealth fund, taxes on automated labor, and a potential four-day workweek to address AI-driven disruption. The company said the rapid advance toward superintelligence requires far-reaching economic reform, including shifting the tax base from labor income to corporate income and capital gains. The proposals also advocate for accelerated grid expansion and automatic unemployment benefits triggered by AI disruption thresholds. Read more →

How to Use the New ChatGPT App Integrations

OpenAI's ChatGPT now integrates with DoorDash, Spotify, Uber, Booking.com, Canva, Figma, and more — letting users order food, build playlists, book rides, and create designs directly from the chat interface. Users connect accounts via Settings and can disconnect anytime. The integrations are currently limited to the U.S. and Canada, with PayPal and Walmart coming later this year. Read more →

Gemini in Google Maps Plans Your Day Surprisingly Well

A Verge reviewer tested Gemini's "Ask Maps" feature for a full day in Seattle, using it to plan a transit itinerary with restaurant, park, and coffee shop stops. The AI pulled from user reviews and real-time data to surface genuinely new spots alongside obvious picks. One hallucination sent the reviewer a block in the wrong direction, but the overall results beat manual searching. Read more →

Anthropic Forms PAC as AI Policy Battles Intensify

Anthropic established a federal Political Action Committee, signaling a shift toward direct political engagement as AI regulation intensifies. The PAC will support candidates aligned with the company's views on AI policy ahead of midterm elections. The move puts Anthropic alongside OpenAI and larger tech firms building out Washington presence, but forming a PAC is unusual for a startup still in growth mode. Read more →

As AI Agents Take on More Tasks, Governance Becomes a Priority

Deloitte research found 23% of companies already use AI agents, with adoption expected to reach 74% within two years — but only 21% report having strong safeguards. As agents move from pilots to production, the firm is developing governance frameworks that define what systems can access, what actions they can take, and how decisions are tracked across the full lifecycle. Japan Pushes Physical AI to Tackle Labor Shortages and Boost Automation

Japan is accelerating deployment of AI-powered robots across industry, with the government targeting 30% of the global physical AI market by 2040. Backed by $6.3 billion in funding, the push responds to labor shortages as the working-age population declines. Companies like Mujin are building software layers for autonomous robot operation, moving beyond pilot programs into real-world logistics and manufacturing. Read more →

Folk Musician Finds AI-Cloned Songs Uploaded Under Her Name on Spotify

Folk musician Murphy Campbell discovered AI-generated versions of her songs uploaded under her name on Spotify, created by cloning her vocals from YouTube videos. Testing confirmed the tracks were likely AI-generated. Her attempts to remove them were complicated by false copyright claims filed by third parties, highlighting gaps in how streaming platforms verify whether uploads come from credited artists. Anthropic Acquires Biotech AI Startup Coefficient Bio in $400M Deal

Anthropic acquired biotech AI startup Coefficient Bio in a $400 million all-stock deal, its largest acquisition to date. The stealth-mode company brings specialized capabilities tied to computational biology. The deal positions Anthropic in a growing area where AI is accelerating biological research, moving the company beyond large language models into specialized applications like drug discovery and protein engineering. Read more →

AI Has a Big Problem When It Comes to Financial Advice

An MIT professor warns that AI chatbots have a fundamental problem with financial advice: models trained on internet-scale data lack the ability to account for individual circumstances, regulatory nuance, and rapidly changing market conditions. The research highlights risks for consumers relying on AI for investment guidance without understanding the limitations of generalized outputs. Read more →

Intel Bets Billions on Chip Packaging to Win AI Race

Intel is betting billions on advanced chip packaging — assembling multiple chiplets into single processors — as the next competitive edge in AI infrastructure. The company's EMIB and Foveros 3D stacking technologies are two to three years ahead of most competitors. With the advanced packaging market projected to exceed $50 billion by 2028, Intel is opening capacity to rivals including Nvidia. Read more →

Xoople Raises $130M to Map the Earth for AI

Spanish geospatial startup Xoople closed a $130 million Series B alongside a partnership with defense contractor L3Harris to build satellite sensors. The deal signals Earth observation data transitioning from government tool to commercial AI infrastructure. As multimodal models increasingly need real-world spatial training data, Xoople positions continuous satellite imagery as foundational alongside text and code. Read more →

🏥 HEALTHCARE

UnitedHealth Group's Massive AI Push Raises Questions for Patients

UnitedHealth Group is embedding AI across its core operations, employing 22,000 software engineers with over 80% now using AI to write code or build agents. The company is automating claims processing, fraud detection, clinical documentation, and billing code selection. While the push promises efficiency, it raises new risks for patients who may not know when or how AI is making decisions about their care. Read more →

Google and Amazon Pour Combined $65B Into Anthropic in the Same Week

Google committed $10B upfront to Anthropic this week, with up to $30B more contingent on performance milestones — at Anthropic's $350B January valuation, even as secondary markets now price the company above $800B. Amazon moved first days earlier with a $25B pledge. The deals are deeply structural: Google's commitment includes 3.5GW of TPU capacity via Broadcom coming online in 2027, 1M TPUs worth tens of billions, and 5GW of Google Cloud compute over five years. With an October IPO reportedly on the horizon, both tech giants appear to be locking in their cap-table positions before public market pricing takes over. Anthropic has now been pledged more than $100B in a single year. Read more →

OpenAI Rewrites Its Founding Principles for the First Time in Eight Years

Sam Altman published five new principles — democratization, empowerment, prosperity, resilience, and adaptability — replacing a 2018 charter that mentioned AGI twelve times. The document now mentions it twice. A 2018 pledge to stop competing with and assist any safety-conscious rival that drew close to AGI has been silently removed. Read more →

NEC becomes Anthropic's first Japan-based global partner, deploying Claude, Claude Opus 4.7, and Claude Code across 30,000+ employees in finance, manufacturing, and local government. NEC will also integrate Claude into its Security Operations Centre and BluStellar Scenario platform. Read more →

China's DeepSeek Releases V4 AI Model to Rival OpenAI and Google at a Fraction of the Price

DeepSeek dropped its V4 model this week, re-entering the frontier race months after V3 rattled Western AI investors. The release lands in the same week as GPT-5.5 and Claude Opus 4.7 — three frontier model releases in a single seven-day window as US-China AI competition intensifies. Read more →

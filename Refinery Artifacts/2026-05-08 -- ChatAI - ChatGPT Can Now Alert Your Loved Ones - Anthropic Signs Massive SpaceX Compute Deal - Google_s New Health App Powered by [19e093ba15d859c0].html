ChatGPT's New 'Trusted Contact' Feature Could Alert Loved Ones in Emergencies

OpenAI is rolling out an optional safety feature called Trusted Contact that lets adult users designate someone who can be alerted if ChatGPT detects conversations indicating serious self-harm risk. Flagged conversations are reviewed by a specialized human safety team — with a target review time under one hour — before any notification is sent. Alerts contain no chat transcripts, only limited context encouraging the contact to check in. The feature was developed with input from the American Psychological Association, suicide prevention organizations, and a Global Physicians Network of 260+ licensed physicians across 60 countries. Anthropic Expands Claude Capacity With Huge SpaceX Compute Deal

Anthropic announced access to SpaceX's Colossus 1 data center — adding 300+ megawatts of compute and 220,000+ NVIDIA GPUs within the next month. The company is doubling Claude Code's rate limits, removing peak-hour usage reductions, and raising API rate limits for Opus models. Anthropic also expressed interest in developing orbital AI compute with SpaceX. The deal adds to existing partnerships with Amazon (5 GW), Google/Broadcom (5 GW), and Microsoft/Nvidia ($30B Azure). Read more →

OpenAI Unveils AI Voice Models That Can Talk, Translate, and Transcribe Live

OpenAI launched three new Realtime API models: GPT-Realtime-2 with GPT-5-class reasoning and a 128K token context window, GPT-Realtime-Translate supporting 70+ input languages, and GPT-Realtime-Whisper for streaming transcription. On Big Bench Audio, GPT-Realtime-2 scored 96.6% accuracy vs. 81.4% for the prior model. Zillow, Priceline, Intercom, and Deutsche Telekom are already testing. Read more →

Perplexity's Personal Computer Turns Your Mac Into an AI Workstation

Perplexity expanded access to Personal Computer, its AI agent system for macOS, now available to all Mac users. The app lets AI agents work across local files, native Mac apps, browser sessions, and 400+ external connectors. Users can initiate tasks remotely from an iPhone and let agents run autonomously in the background on always-on devices like Mac mini. Read more →

Google Pulls the Plug on Project Mariner AI Assistant

Google shut down Project Mariner, its experimental AI agent that browsed the web and completed tasks autonomously. The technology, first introduced in December 2024 through Google Labs, has been folded into Gemini Agent. The 17-month lifespan follows a familiar Google Labs pattern where experimental products are absorbed into larger platforms. Read more →

New in Claude Managed Agents: Dreaming, Outcomes, and Multiagent Orchestration

Anthropic launched "dreaming" in Claude Managed Agents — a scheduled process that reviews past sessions, extracts patterns, and curates memories so agents self-improve over time. Also shipping: outcomes (agents self-grade against rubrics with up to 10-point task success improvements), multiagent orchestration, and webhooks for asynchronous workflows. Read more →

Google's New Health App Uses Gemini AI for Fitness and Medical Insights

Google is rebranding Fitbit as the Google Health app, consolidating fitness tracking with a Gemini-powered Health Coach that delivers personalized workout plans, sleep analysis, and medical record summaries. Rolling out May 19 as an automatic update. Google also introduced the Fitbit Air wearable and a Google Health Premium subscription for deeper AI coaching. The company reiterated it won't use health data for Google Ads. Read more →

Samsung's Galaxy Watch May Warn Users Before They Faint

A peer-reviewed clinical study found the Galaxy Watch6 predicted fainting episodes up to five minutes in advance with 84.6% accuracy. The system uses photoplethysmography sensors and AI to analyze heart rate variability patterns associated with vasovagal syncope. Published in the European Heart Journal – Digital Health, it's one of the first demonstrations of a commercial smartwatch detecting warning signs before a medical event. Read more →

OpenAI Unveils GPT-5.5-Cyber Amid Growing AI Cybersecurity Race

OpenAI is rolling out GPT-5.5-Cyber in limited preview for vetted cybersecurity teams. The model is more permissive for defensive security workflows — including vulnerability identification, malware analysis, red teaming, and exploit verification — while blocking credential theft and malware deployment. Partners include Cisco, CrowdStrike, Palo Alto Networks, and Cloudflare. Starting June 1, users must enable phishing-resistant account protections. Read more →

Anthropic Launches Research Institute to Study AI's Real-World Impact

Anthropic published the research agenda for The Anthropic Institute (TAI), a new body studying AI's economic, security, and societal impacts from inside a frontier lab. Focus areas include economic diffusion (who benefits from AI adoption), threats and resilience (dual-use capabilities, offense-defense balance), AI systems in the wild (group epistemology, critical thinking degradation), and AI-driven R&D (recursive self-improvement risks). NASA Deploys First AI Foundation Model in Orbit: Researchers successfully deployed NASA and IBM's open-source Prithvi geospatial AI model to two platforms in space — the Kanyini satellite and the International Space Station — making it the first foundation model ever run in orbit. The model processes Earth observation data directly in space rather than transmitting raw data back to ground stations, enabling faster disaster response, environmental monitoring, and climate analysis. It marks a milestone in bringing AI compute to the edge of Earth's atmosphere.

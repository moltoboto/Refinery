New AI Agent 'Poke' Brings Automation to iPhone Messages and SMS

Poke, a startup backed by Spark Capital and General Catalyst, has launched a consumer AI agent that works through iMessage, SMS, and Telegram — letting users automate everyday tasks without downloading an app. Users can set up medication reminders, track fitness goals, monitor emails, control smart home devices, and manage calendars, all by texting. The service routes tasks to whichever AI model fits best, using a mix of commercial and open-source options. Poke also offers prebuilt "recipes" that connect to services like Gmail, Notion, Strava, Oura, and GitHub. Signups have increased tenfold in recent months, and the company has added $10 million in new funding on top of a $15 million seed round, bringing its post-money valuation to $300 million. Microsoft Removes Copilot From Core Windows 11 Apps in Major UI Shift

Microsoft is scaling back Copilot integration across Windows 11, removing AI branding from Notepad and pulling Copilot buttons from Snipping Tool entirely. The company acknowledged it pushed the feature too aggressively and plans to cut "unnecessary Copilot entry points" across apps including Photos and Widgets. AI tools remain available but are being regrouped under more neutral labels like "Advanced Features." Read more →

Google's Gemini AI Can Now Answer Questions With 3D Models and Simulations

Google upgraded Gemini to generate interactive 3D models and simulations in response to prompts. Users can rotate models, adjust sliders, and input different values to change simulations in real time. The update arrives weeks after Anthropic gave Claude auto-generated charts and OpenAI added math visualizations to ChatGPT. Available now via the "Pro" model in the Gemini app. Read more →

Button AI Device Debuts as a Simpler Alternative to Failed AI Wearables

Former Apple Vision Pro engineers Chris Nolet and Ryan Burgoyne unveiled Button, a $179 handheld AI device that activates only when pressed — no passive listening or always-on microphones. The aluminum puck connects to earbuds or smart glasses via Bluetooth and responds to queries almost instantly. The founders position it as a privacy-first alternative to failed AI wearables like the Humane Ai Pin. Read more →

Google Bets on Intel Xeon CPUs to Boost AI Training and Inference

Google is expanding its partnership with Intel to deploy multiple generations of Xeon CPUs in its AI data centers for both training and inference workloads. Intel CEO Lip-Bu Tan framed the deal around "balanced systems" rather than reliance on GPUs alone. The companies are also collaborating on an infrastructure processing unit designed to offload networking and storage tasks. Read more →

Meta Launches Muse Spark AI Model With Multimodal Reasoning and Multi-Agent Capabilities

Meta launched Muse Spark, the first model from its Superintelligence Labs, marking a reset of its AI strategy after Llama 4. The natively multimodal model processes text, images, audio, and video, and supports multi-agent coordination where separate AI agents handle different parts of a task in parallel. Available now in the Meta AI app with rollout planned across Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp. Read more →

Google Launches Gemini Notebooks to Rival ChatGPT Projects

Google launched Notebooks for Gemini, a feature that organizes files, past conversations, and custom instructions into persistent workspaces. The system automatically references stored materials when users interact with Gemini inside a notebook, reducing the need to rebuild context across sessions. The release arrives more than a year after OpenAI introduced a similar system with ChatGPT Projects. Read more →

Google Adds AI Booking Feature to Search, Letting Users Reserve Tables Instantly

Google is rolling out agentic capabilities in AI Mode within Search in the U.K., letting users describe dining preferences in natural language and receive restaurant options with real-time availability. AI Mode aggregates bookings across platforms including TheFork, Sevenrooms, and DesignMyNight, moving users from discovery to reservation in a few steps. Cloudflare Takes on WordPress With New AI-Powered CMS EmDash

Cloudflare launched EmDash, an open-source CMS designed to let AI agents handle tasks like updating content and adjusting layouts with minimal human input. Positioned as a "spiritual successor" to WordPress, the platform drew an immediate response from WordPress founder Matt Mullenweg. EmDash enters a market where WordPress powers roughly 43% of websites globally, but faces significant migration hurdles. Read more →

🎬 ENTERTAINMENT

YouTube Shorts Launches AI Avatar Cloning for Creators

YouTube Shorts is rolling out an AI-powered avatar feature that lets creators build digital clones of themselves for video content — no studio required. Creators can drop their AI twin into existing videos or generate entirely new ones. Videos using avatars will carry AI disclosures, and creators can limit remixes. The launch follows CEO Neal Mohan's earlier hints about AI likeness tools coming in 2026. Read more →

Alibaba's HappyHorse AI Video Model Tops Global Ranking on Debut

Alibaba confirmed it built HappyHorse, an AI video generation model that topped the Artificial Analysis text-to-video leaderboard on its debut, surpassing ByteDance's Seedance 2.0 and Google's Veo. The 15-billion-parameter model is the first open-source video model with native joint audio-video generation and supports seven languages with lip-sync. Alibaba plans to release the model weights on GitHub. Read more →

Perplexity Adds Plaid Integration to Track Bank Accounts, Credit Cards, and Loans

Perplexity launched a Plaid integration that lets U.S. and Canadian users connect bank accounts, credit cards, and loans directly to its AI platform. The system can process transactions, monitor balances, and generate tools like budget trackers and net worth dashboards through natural language queries. Over 75% of Perplexity's users already engage with financial queries monthly. Data access is read-only through Plaid. Read more →

Meta Commits $21B to CoreWeave in One of AI's Largest Infrastructure Contracts

Meta committed $21 billion to CoreWeave for dedicated AI cloud capacity through December 2032, one of the largest infrastructure contracts in the industry. Combined with a previous $14.2 billion agreement, the deals are worth more than $35 billion. The contract focuses on inference workloads for Meta's Llama-based models across Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Messenger. CoreWeave shares rose about 8% on the news. Read more →

🏥 HEALTHCARE

OpenAI Foundation Commits $100M+ in Grants to Accelerate Alzheimer's Research

The OpenAI Foundation announced more than $100 million in grants across six research institutions to accelerate Alzheimer's research using AI. Partners include Arc Institute, the Institute for Protein Design, UCSF, and EvE Bio. The initiative spans five areas: mapping causal factors, designing new drugs, building open datasets, establishing biomarkers, and testing off-patent treatments. The foundation calls it "a clear test of AI's ability to change what is possible in human health." Read more →

Fitbit AI Health Coach Goes Global With 37-Country Rollout

Google expanded Fitbit's AI-powered personal health coach from a limited preview to 37 countries and 32 languages, adding VO2 Max cardio fitness tracking. The AI analyzes sleep patterns, activity levels, heart rate, and cardiovascular performance to deliver personalized recommendations. The expansion puts Google in direct competition with Apple Health and Samsung's AI fitness tools. Fitbit Premium costs $9.99 per month. Read more →
